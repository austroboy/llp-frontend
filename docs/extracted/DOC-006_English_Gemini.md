No. 46 (P. & P.)—The following Ordinance, made by the President of the People's Republic of Bangladesh on 02 Agrahayan, 1432 BS / 17 November, 2025 AD, is hereby published for general information.
Ordinance No. 46, 2025
Made for the purpose of further amending the Bangladesh Labour Act, 2006
Ordinance
Whereas it is expedient and necessary to further amend the Bangladesh Labour Act, 2006 (Act No. 42 of 2006) for the purpose of fulfilling the objectives stated below; and
Whereas Parliament stands dissolved and it has satisfactorily appeared to the President that circumstances exist which render it necessary to take immediate action;
Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by Article 93(1) of the Constitution of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, the President is pleased to make and promulgate the following Ordinance:
1. Short title and commencement.—(1) This Ordinance may be called the Bangladesh Labour (Amendment) Ordinance, 2025.

“61A. Method of Work. —(1) No work or production process shall be carried out and no goods shall be stored in any establishment in such a manner as may cause physical injury.

(2) No employer shall compel any worker to perform such dangerous work if the worker informs his superior that the method of work may pose a serious danger to his life or health.

(3) No employer shall take any disciplinary action or any other unwarranted measure against any worker if the worker takes personal protective measures in accordance with this Act and the Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) policy and removes himself from any situation about which he has reasonable cause to believe that it will create an imminent and serious danger.

(4) If worker representatives take any appropriate action in accordance with existing other laws and policies, no employer shall take any disciplinary action or any other unwarranted measure against them.

(5) If work is stopped due to occupational safety risks, the employer may, if he wishes, engage the worker in any other relatively safe work, provided that his wages and other conditions remain the same.”
